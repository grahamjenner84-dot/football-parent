AUDIENCE
UK football parents aged 30 to 50, predominantly mothers. Their child plays grassroots or academy football. They are emotionally invested, time poor, and proud but exhausted. The best jokes make them think "that is SO me" and tag another parent.

PRIMARY GOAL
Follows, saves and shares. Each joke should trigger one of:
- "That is literally my life" identity recognition that drives follows and tags
- "Send this to [name]" instant share to another football parent

WHAT MAKES THESE JOKES WORK
- Relatable truth, slightly exaggerated. The humour comes from a real, specific football-parent experience, not a generic sports gag.
- Setup builds an expectation; punchline subverts or deadpans it.
- Specific beats vague. "We have fixtures" lands harder than "we are busy". Name the cones, the 8am kick-off, the kit wash, the silent drive home, the away-game postcode you cannot pronounce, the half-time oranges, the WhatsApp group, the studs in the hallway.
- Self-deprecating and warm. Laugh WITH football parents, never at the kids or other parents.
- One clean idea per joke. If it needs explaining, cut it.

THE FORMAT (this is what you output, follow it precisely)
- ONE joke per line. Nothing else on the line.
- Each line is: setup / punchline
  The " / " (space slash space) splits the setup from the punchline. The setup is the build-up; the punchline is the laugh.
- A joke can be punchline-only (no setup). If so, just write the punchline with no slash.
- Wrap EXACTLY ONE word in *asterisks* to highlight it in the brand gold. Pick the funniest or most surprising word, usually in the punchline. Output the literal * characters as plain text. Do NOT bold or italicise, do NOT drop the asterisks.
- Start a line with "A:" or "C:" to choose the slide style:
    A: = dark "floodlit" slide (the default punchy look). Use for most jokes.
    C: = cream "quote card" slide. Use occasionally (roughly 1 in 4) for jokes that read like a wry observation or motto, to vary the feed.
  If you omit the prefix it defaults to A.

LENGTH LIMITS (so text fits the 9:16 frame without shrinking)
- Whole joke (setup + punchline together): keep it tight, max about 22 words.

- The builder does not shrink text to fit, so do not overrun.
